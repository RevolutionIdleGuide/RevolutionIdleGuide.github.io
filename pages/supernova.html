Intro

You have reached Supernova! This is the beginning of the long grind towards your next major goal. Before you get into the guide, there is some important and useful information to note below.

You should avoid any of the following actions:

  • Right clicking an upgrade that cost Solar Shards.
  • Not Spending your Solar Flares when the cap is full.

Guide Info & Formatting

This guide will use abbreviations, with the first use of them being marked in bold next to the full version i.e. Supernova Tier (SNT). Names of resets and currencies will also be marked in bold for clarity.

SNT1

Welcome to the Supernova (SN) realm! Right now there are only 2 boards: Solar Upgrades and Supernova Tiers. The Solar Upgrades is where you buy upgrades while the Supernova Tier board shows your Supernova Tier (SNT) and the boosts you get from it.

You’ll have 3 unlocked tabs on the Solar Upgrades Board. The first two is where you spend Solar Shards while the last one is where you spend Solar Flares (SF). SF are a currency that passively generates. The first SF upgrade you should buy is The Solar Idler, then buy whatever you want after that. The Solar Idler generates Solar Shards every 24 hours.

Note: SF Generation is based on the number of SN you have done and the Star Tier that you did it at. You get 10x the SF Generation per Star Tier.

You should buy Solar Shard upgrades in this order:

  • Solar Powered (SoP) Grass 1/5000
  • SoP Steel 1/50
  • Constant RF
  • Constant Rings
  • SoP Lines 1/500
  • Orbit
  • Advanced Constellations 1/4
Note 1: SoP stands for Solar Powered.
Note 2: After buying Constant RF, make sure you turn off RF production.

Recovery

Recovery is relatively simple. You’ll easily breeze through early game, just don’t forget to do the empower accomplishment. The Rocket Launch Pad only requires 1 rocket part instead of 10, so galactic right away!

Tip: Have an autoclicker for spamming right click to quickly max buy upgrades.

Get AGH7 next. Don’t forget to unlock Funify and buy SFRGT and Charger Mk II. Now aim for AGHGS 3, then get AGHGS 12. AGHGS 18 isn’t required to unlock the Unnatural realm, so get that ASAP. The huge unnatural grass boost should easily help you unlock Vaporizer and push you very close to Planetoid.

Planetoid will be pretty much the same except faster. Make sure to get Keep Perks..? on Astro before AGHGS 45.

Unlock Planetary Trials and PT8 first before you unlock the Lunar Obelisk (LO). Then use the boost from LO to unlock Constellations again. Because best Rings is updated automatically, you only need to have your ring constellation setup active very briefly.

Note: Best Ring is not updated if your constellation is unstable.

Get PT16 and then use that boost to get GJ 18. After unlocking Dark Charge, get AGHGS 60, then progress until you get Planetary Trial level 1600. Keep on improving your Constellations until you reach GJ 35.

DO NOT SUPERNOVA AGAIN AT STAR TIER 1.

Note 1: Once you are getting close to SN again, start spending all of your SF on Star Growth, Stardust, and Solar Shard, as waiting for star tier 2 takes a while.
Note 2: You automatically unlock the new Observatorium upgrades from GJ 40 after doing your first SN.

Pushing to the Next Tier

This part of Supernova is pretty tedious and long. You can choose two ways.

Option 1: Grind up star growth until it will take 2 days to reach tier 2. Then go afk and check back every few hours to spend the stardust and your solar flares. You may also obtain Solar Shards from your Solar Idler during this time.

Option 2 (the better option): Push your constellation and arc tiers, Grass Jumps and Planetary Trials. Repeat this and farm Planetarys and Planetoid Level. This is because Planetoid level boosts Stardust generation and you can earn more levels through higher compaction.

Note: Once you get to T2 Star, it will say that T3 Star requires 10 eclipses. This can only be obtainable after reaching SNT2, so don’t go for T3.

If you choose option 2, be aware that you may be able to push for Astral Prestiges 7 or 8+, boosting stardust further.

Eclipse

SNT2 unlocks Eclipse, which is like a leveling system in the SN realm. Eclipse can be increased by obtaining Solar Rays. Solar rays can only be obtained by performing Supernovas. The amount of Solar Rays you get is based off of your Star growth’s OoM and the GJ55 Milestone.

You also get Remnants every time you level up your Eclipse. Spend your first Remnant on Solar Shards. (If you got two, spend it on Star Growth). Star growth is going to be the most important, but once you get more, you can start spending some on Solar Shards and SF.

Note: The SN reset board currently displays the wrong number of Solar Rays and Eclipses that you will get. You will get more than what it says.

Prism

Prism is a constellation that combines lines, rings, moons, bolts, and arcs together into one constellation. Prisms generate the same amount of instability as line generators. However, they cost 1000x more lines, so it’s recommended to also have a line generator that is one tier higher than the prism to generate more lines.

SNT3

SNT3 unlocks an extremely important boost: Anti Xp and Unnatural Xp are multiplied by Normal Xp instead of being boosted at a reduced rate. This means that you will have significantly more Anti and Unnatural Xp, which allows you to unlock the Star almost immediately and be able to get way more Solar Rays from the GJ55 and 100 milestone.

Buy these solar shard upgrades in this order:

  • Starting Rings 12/12 (1397 shards) or buy as much as you can, just make sure you get at least 6/12 (274 shards)
  • Mega Dark Fruits
  • Auto Rocket
  • SoP Fun 1/150
  • Filler Star dust 1k/1k
  • Filler Solar Flares 200/1k
  • Solar Flare Capacity 2-4/25
  • SoP Lines 3-4/500
  • SoP Arcs 2/500
Note: SoP stands for Solar Powered.
Note 2: Keep in mind Mega Dark Fruits does not automate Dark Accelerator upgrades.

The huge amount of GJ should also be enough for you to get way past Eclipse 95. Try to aim for 20k+ solar shards on SNT4.

SNT4

SNT4 has a new upgrade that unlocks Persistent Constellation. What makes it unique is that Persistent Constellations DO NOT RESET on a supernova. However, it costs 1QaQd (1e135) lines.

SNT4 also unlocks a new reset called Sunrise, but you can ignore it until you have a Persistent Constellation in your Constellation.

You should buy Solar Shard upgrades in this order:

  • Starting Rings 12/12 (this should definitely be maxed by now)
  • Auto Rocket
  • Starting Star Chat 10/10
  • Dark Accelerator Automation
  • Funny Machine Automation
  • Fun-dry Automation
  • Filler Solar Flares 1k/1k
  • Solar Flare Capacity 7/25
  • Advanced Constellations 4/4
  • SoP lines 9/500 (~73.8k shards, do not skip this upgrade)
  • Last Prism
  • Constellation Grid
  • Ray
  • Star
  • Improver
  • SoP arcs 3-4/500

Persistents

In order to get 1QaQd lines to afford the Last Prism, every challenge in the Planetary Trial to be maxed and the ring upgrade “Lines” should be maxed too. SoP Lines should be at level 9, SoP Lines II should be at level 23, and SoP Lines III should be at level 1.

Late Prism Setup:

Note: Be aware that you are meant to upgrade the tiers of the constellations rather than use the base tier shown. Different configurations will be minimally better with prisms at this stage.

Make sure both of these constellation setups are saved. Use line generators 1 tier higher than your prism. You will most definitely need to use a time warp on the final push.

Note 1: If your line generation is still low, replace a reinforcement and a bulwark with a magnifier.
Note 2: If you are halfway there to 1QaQd lines, you can do a constellation reset which essentially doubles your produced lines.

Once you have 1QaQd lines, place the Last Prism anywhere in your constellation. This will persist through SN and all other non-persistent constellation buildings will be reset.

Don’t Supernova yet because you should do a sunrise first.

Sunrise

Sunrise is a game mechanic where you ONLY reset your Eclipse for Sunstone and an increased multiplier for Solar Shards and SF cap.

Before you do your first Sunrise, make sure you have a T4 Star prepared, but don’t Supernova. Your first Sunrise should be done for bare minimum 100 sunstone. After Sunrising, buy 1 level of Sunstone Remnant and the rest on Star Growth. You can Supernova after doing that.

Note: You only need to Sunrise once in this guide.

Once you have unlocked every Persistent Constellation, use the setup below.

Early Persistent Constellation:

Pushing to Eclipse 265 will require you to do multiple SN. You will need to max Solar Rays I and Solar Rays II in the SF tab, and preferably have at least 100+ levels in Solar Rays Filler. Do your first T5 SN for any amount of Solar Shards.

SNT5

SNT5 unlocks the final obelisk in the Light Forest: The Void Obelisk. This obelisk allows you to centralize a currency and gives you a singularity in return.

Note 1: You should always centralize currencies as soon as you can, since it’s required to progress and there are no downsides to it. The order that you centralize currency should be the order arranged in the void obelisk, from top to bottom.
Note 2: Centralizing a currency does not delete it, it just sets the currency to a set value and makes the currency and the buildings of the currency hidden.

Get ASP 13 and SoP Prestige level 5 and that should be enough to centralize Prestige Points. Go back to the Star and spend your first singularity on Solarians.

Congratulations for making it to the end of this guide!
